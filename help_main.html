PURPOSE:

The LOCATION ASSIST SERVICE (the App) is a navigation aide aimed at hikers, explorers or boaters that uses received Global Positioning System (GPS) signals to:

-          provide various running and summary statistics of the user’s trip as a label floating on top of other applications

-          graphically display a collection of locations the user has been visiting (crumbs)

-          create waypoints with current or any GPS location and display them on a current track

-          aide a user to follow a preloaded track or reaching a waypoint by showing the user’s location and a compass

-          playback the current track in the same sequence it was created

It also allows provides GPX files managing capabilities such as:

-          importing external GPX files and converting them into tracks to be displayed by the app

-          converting user’s tracks into GPX files and vice versa

-          sharing user’s GPX files via e-mail, messenger, etc. to be imported into external applications such as Google Maps

(GPX or GPS exchange format, is an XML file format for storing coordinate data. It can store waypoints, tracks, and routes in a way that is easy to process and convert to other forms)

Finally it allows saving trips summaries as text for further viewing and sharing via e-mail, messenger, etc.

The App is especially useful in the areas such as open water or a forest where a traditional map is of a very limited use. The App is implemented as a service so it can keep collecting data even when running in the background. Note that presently the App does not show the map on the track screen – it might be added in the future.

 

DEFINITIONS:

TRIP – various statistics captured between clicking ‘Start’ and ‘Stop’ on the Settings screen provided at least one GPS location was registered

TRACK – a collection of GPS locations (CRUMBS) gathered during a TRIP. Can be backed up; named and stored in the device memory; can be exported as part of a GPX file and displayed in Google Maps and other software that can consume GPX file format. A TRACK can be imported into the App and converted into the internal format. Thus a user can have a TRACK of a particular hike to follow before going hiking.  TRACK also can be played back (animated) to see its natural progression. TRACK can be backed up automatically every 50 CRUMBS.

CRUMB - a GPS location that includes latitude, longitude, timestamp and altitude (if available). Collection of CRUMBS (a TRACK) is displayed on the Track Screen and can be followed by the user to find the way back or to a desired WAYPOINT

WAYPOINT - a named CRUMB whose name is displayed on the Track Screen and can be stored in the device memory and exported as part of a GPX file and displayed in Google Maps and other software that can consume GPX file format

FLOATING LABEL - a user-sized overlay window that floats on top of other running applications or a home screen on your device and displays various statistics of your TRIP. Can have a transparent or a solid white background. Can be hidden or relocated to any position on the screen for convenience

Any combination of the following parameters can be displayed and changed while the App is running.

-          Current latitude, longitude and altitude (if altitude reading is available in hardware on your device)

-          Difference between current and starting altitude (point-to-point or P2P altitude change)

-          Lowest, highest  trip altitudes and their difference (altitude range)

-          Current elevation angle

-          Average elevation angle of the trip

-          Current speed over a two dimensional surface (does not account for altitude)

-          Current 3-D speed over a three-dimensional surface (accounts for altitude)

-          Average speed over a two dimensional surface (does not account for altitude)

-          Average 3-D speed over a three-dimensional surface (accounts for altitude)

-          Total covered distance over a two dimensional surface (does not account for altitude)

-          Total covered 3-D distance over a three dimensional surface (accounts for altitude)

-          Straight distance between starting and current point over a two dimensional surface (P2P distance)

-          Last and average bearing

-          Trip duration (time between the first error-free GPS reading and current time)

APPLICATION PERMISSIONS AND REQUIREMENTS

In order for the App to perform its functions the following permissions must be granted: ‘Location’ and ‘Permit drawing over other apps’. The App will request those permissions automatically upon first run. The App has been tested on Android versions 11, 12 and 13. Your device also must have a GPS receiver.

 

QUICK OPERATION SUMMARY

Push a button labeled ‘H’ in the upper right corner on other screens of the App for a detailed description of the operations on that screen

 

TRIP Operation  (Configuration  screen)

1)      Clicking ‘Start’ begins the TRIP data collection and puts the App in the ONLINE mode.  A FLOATING LABEL will appear with the information you configure to view via ‘Options’ selector

2)      Clicking ‘Stop’ finishes the TRIP data collection and puts the App in the OFFLINE mode. The user will be presented with a Trip Summary window and options to share and/or save it

3)      When the App is in ONLINE mode the ‘Pause’ button becomes enabled.  Clicking on it puts the App in the PAUSED mode – it stops listening on the GPS location (pause time does not count in the stats). Now the button reads ‘Resume’

4)      When the App is in PAUSED mode clicking on ‘Resume’ button puts the App in the ONLINE mode and the data collection resumes

5)      ‘Accuracy’ option means the accepted accuracy of the GPS receiver in your device to register a location. The smaller the value is – the more locations will be rejected by the device’s GPS receiver as inaccurate. A 10-meter accuracy is sufficient for most applications

6)      ‘Rate’ option means the interval in seconds between two successive GPS locations received by the App. For hiking 10 seconds is sufficient, for driving or boating the rate should be smaller for more precise tracking

7)      Clicking ‘Trips’ on the Main screen opens the ‘Saved trips’ screen where the user can view/delete/share summaries for each saved TRIP

8)      Clicking on the floating label at any time shows running summary of the TRIP

 

FLOATING LABEL

1)      Shows the TRIP information (configured via ‘Options’ on the Configuration screen)

2)      Floats on top of any other running application

3)      Shows a non-magnetic calculated (based on two last GPS locations) bearing in the upper left corner via a compass image ⎋when available or X when not available

4)      To hide, click on the compass image  in the upper left corner of the LABEL, to show – click on the image again

5)      To relocate , drag the LABEL to the desired location on the screen with your finger

6)      To see the running summary of your TRIP, click on the FLOATING LABEL text

7)      To change the size of the label use a ‘Label size’ selector on top of the Configuration screen

8)      To change Distance measurement units (meters, kilometers, feet, yards, miles, nautical miles) use a ‘Distance Units’ selector on the Configuration screen

9)      To change Speed measurement units (meters per second, kilometers per hour, feet per second, miles per hour, knots) use a ‘Speed Units’ selector on the Configuration screen

 

TRACKS Operations

1)      Clicking ‘Tracks’ opens the TRACK screen where CRUMBS collected along your route are displayed. The user will be presented with an option to load the last backed-up or saved TRACK (either named or unnamed)

2)      To get back to the Configuration screen use the ‘Back’ button on your device. Tracking will continue

3)      Pushing a Menu button (▤)in the upper left corner of the TRACK screen shows a TRACKS menu with the following options:

-          ‘Backup current track’ – backs up the current TRACK (CRUMBS and WAYPOINTS) in the internal binary format. If the file had been named and saved prior – it would be saved to the same file. Otherwise it would be saved as an ‘Unnamed’ file. Note that the ‘Unnamed’ file cannot be converted to GPX format and shared.  Moreover, it will be overwritten on the next app run. So it is recommended to save it as a named file on the ‘Manage TRACKS’ screen.

-          ‘Playback current track’ – animates the TRACK. After the animation is finished, use Ⓡ button in the upper right corner to replay or ☒ button to get out of the playback mode

-          ‘Clear crumbs – clears collected crumbs but will accept new ones

-          ‘Manage tracks’ -  takes you to the TRACK editing screen

-          ‘Edit GPX Files’ - takes you to the GPX files  editing screen

-          ‘Manage Track Waypoints’ – takes you to the TRACK WAYPOINTS editing screen

-          ‘Manage Global Waypoints’ – takes you to the Global WAYPOINTS editing screen

4)      CRUMBS are collected at the distance of no less than 10 meters from each other. Last CRUMB is shown as red circle of a large diameter, first crumb – in black

5)      TRACK screen shows compass – it is not a magnetic compass and determines the bearing based on the last two collected GPS locations

6)      Zooming in ⊕, out ⊖ and restoring to the original size ⊜ are supported via semi-transparent icons in the lower right corner. If Zoom IN level is more than one – drag is also supported. The current size of the shown earth surface fragment is shown at the top of the screen and reflects the current zoom level. Zooming OUT is restricted to the original zoom level and only enabled if more than one CRUMB is collected. Zooming in is restricted to approximately 10 meter per screen width

7)      Apps status (ONLINE, OFFLINE, PAUSED) along with the current TRACK’s name are displayed in the lower left corner of the screen

 

Manage TRACKS Screen                (Push a button labeled ‘H’ in the upper right corner on other screens of the App for a detailed description of the operations on that screen)

-           ‘Current: Save as’. A name must be provided.  Creates and saves a new file with the current crumbs. Current TRACK will assume a name of the newly created file 

-          ‘Current: Replace with Selected’. Crumbs of the current TRACK will be replaced with the crumbs of the selected TRACK.  Current TRACK will assume the name of the selected TRACK

-          ‘Current: Merge with Selected’. Crumbs of the current TRACK will be appended to the crumbs of the selected TRACK.  Current TRACK will retain its name

-          ‘Selected: Save as GPX’. Saves (converts) selected TRACK into GPX format so it can be viewed with appropriate software. Name of the created GPX file will appear on “Manage GPX Files” screen

-          ‘Selected: Delete. Deletes selected TRACK. If the current TRACK is deleted – it becomes “!Unnamed!” and current crumbs are NOT affected

-          To get back to the Track Screen use the ‘Back’ button on your device

Manage GPX Files Screen            (Push a button labeled ‘H’ in the upper right corner on other screens of the App for a detailed description of the operations on that screen)

-          ‘Selected: Delete. Deletes selected GPX file. Corresponding TRACK (if any) is NOT affected

-          -‘Selected: Share’. Allows sharing the selected GPX file as an attachment via any software installed on your device such as Email, WhatsApp, etc. If a File Manager is installed – the file can be copied to any public directory such as Download or DCIM 

-          -‘Selected: Convert’. Converts the selected GPX file into a TRACK that can be displayed on the “Track” screen. Name of the converted TRACK will appear on the “Manage Tracks” screen.

-          -‘Import External’. Gives an opportunity to copy a GPX file from any public directory such as Download or DCIM  on your device into the App. The copied file can be further converted via ‘Selected: Convert’ to be used by the App provided its format is proper.  If the external file has more than one track in it only the first one will be converted. If it has Routes in it – only the first one will be converted into TRACK.

-          To get back to the Track Screen use the ‘Back’ button on your device

Manage TRACK Waypoints screen           (Push a button labeled ‘H’ in the upper right corner on other screens of the App for a detailed description of the operations on that screen)

-          ‘Add’. Creates a Waypoint with the current GPS location and adds it to the current TRACK. This function is disabled if the App is in Offline mode. After Waypoint is created it will be seen on the “Tracks” screen – the view will be extended if necessary so the Waypoint would be visible. A waypoint name must be supplied.

-          ‘Delete’. Deletes a selected Waypoint from the Current TRACK.

 

Manage Global Waypoints screen           (Push a button labeled ‘H’ in the upper right corner on other screens of the App for a detailed description of the operations on that screen)

-          ‘Add Specific Location’. Creates a Global Waypoint with the specified latitude and longitude that can be typed in in the fields provided. Up to 6 decimal digits are allowed. A waypoint name must be supplied.

-          ‘Add Current Location’. Creates a Global Waypoint with the current GPS location. This function is disabled if the App is in Offline mode. A waypoint name must be supplied.

-          ‘Delete’. Deletes a selected Global Waypoint.

-          ‘Attach to Current Track’. Adds a selected Global Waypoint to the current TRACK. After Waypoint is added it will be seen on the “Tracks” screen – the view will be extended if necessary so the Waypoint would be visible. A Waypoint name will also appear in the ListView  on  the “Manage Track Waypoints” screen

 

TIPS

1)      Every device has a different GPS receiver. A larger device is more likely to be more sensitive since it can house a larger antenna. The user should vary the ‘Accuracy’ parameter to figure out what works for their device. The information label shows ‘Low accuracy: N’ where N is the actual accuracy of the last received location. If this value is consistently larger than the accuracy set on the Configuration screen, increase the accuracy value to start collecting data.

2)      When moving with a slow speed (pedestrian or a jogger) the current speed values are not very reliable on a device with a bad sensitivity such as a pretty much any smart phone. The higher is your data acquisition rate – the lower the speed accuracy is since the distance between two successive locations could be smaller than the measurement error. Average speed values are pretty accurate

3)      Some GPS receivers do not provide the altitude readings and in general altitude readings are not very reliable

4)      A CRUMB Track screen shows compass. It is not a magnetic compass – the angle is calculated based on two last locations. The reason behind that is twofold. Firstly, most smart phones do not have a magnetic compass. Secondly, even if they do – they are not accurate. Thus a user must be moving to have a reliable compass reading. Reading of the CRUMB track compass can be different from the FLOATING LABEL compass because the FLOATING LABEL compass update rate is what the user selected on the Configuration Screen and the CRUMB Track Screen compass is updated every 10 meters or more.

5)      Update Rate should be selected based upon the speed of movement. The faster you move – the higher the rate should be

6)      Application performs fine in the ‘Airplane’ mode – there is no need for a cellular connection. Only the time of the first data fix might be affected. Actually turning of the cellular radio on your device will be beneficial for saving battery.

7)      Pausing or stopping the TRIP  will disable a GPS receiver and save battery (unless some other application will be using a GPS receiver at the same time)
